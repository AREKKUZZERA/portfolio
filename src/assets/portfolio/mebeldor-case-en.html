A premium landing page for furniture shaped around the interior

MebelDor needed a landing page that sells trust around a major purchase, not a list of products: a clear path from measurement to installation, visual proof of quality and a calm premium tone without pressure.

SegmentBespoke furniture, Moscow region

01 / Problem

A complex service had to be explained in one screen

A bespoke furniture purchase starts with trust, not with a button. The client does not know the final price yet, but still needs confidence in the process: measurement, design, materials, production and installation. The page is built around control, quality and calm confidence instead of discounts and loud promises.

01
No ready-made productEvery kitchen, wardrobe or storage system is designed for a specific room, so the site has to show individuality without uncertainty.
02
Long decision cycleThe user must understand measurement, design, materials, production, delivery and installation before leaving a request.
03
Premium without noiseA dark stage, warm typography and large photographs work harder than overloaded promotions and aggressive CTAs.
Photography as proofThe project uses real furniture images to show material, scale and interior context.
02 / Strategy

The structure moves from trust to request

The visitor first reads the brand level, then sees proof of quality, chooses a relevant direction and understands what happens after the request. The CTA appears only after the page has given the user a reason to trust.

01

First screen as a trust showcase

The hero shows the furniture level, the calm brand tone and the core idea: each solution is designed around the client's interior.

02

Proof before choice

Experience, warranty, own production and free measurement appear before the catalog to reduce key doubts before the request.

03

A form without pressure

The request feels like the start of a consultation: choose a furniture type, leave contact details and get a clear next step.

03 / Visual system

A dark furniture gallery with a warm accent

The palette makes photos feel more expensive: deep black surfaces, cream typography and a restrained gold accent for lines, numbers and calls to action.

The typography separates emotion and interface: the serif display face creates a premium tone, while the geometric UI type keeps navigation, forms and cards structured.

04 / Screens

Key landing page blocks

Each screen handles a separate objection: see the quality, understand the offer, check the process and submit a request without extra steps.

First screen

A strong image-led opening with a short offer and restrained commands.

Catalog

The catalog shows order types, not endless SKU cards: kitchens, wardrobes, storage systems and children rooms.

Process and request

A five-step flow lowers anxiety: request, measurement, design, production and installation.

05 / Responsive

The mobile version keeps the showroom feeling

The hero becomes a vertical interface: the image remains dominant while the copy and CTA avoid covering key furniture details.
Catalog and process cards keep stable proportions, so the screen feels designed instead of becoming a random feed.
The request form stays short: furniture type, name, phone and comment.

06 / Content logic

The case covers the full request path

The English version now keeps the same depth as the Russian presentation: it explains why the user trusts the brand, how categories are selected, how the production process works and why the request form stays short.

01

Trust layer

Experience, warranty, free measurement and own production are placed before the catalog so the visitor sees proof before choosing a furniture type.

02

Category layer

The catalog is organized around real order scenarios: kitchens, wardrobes, storage systems and children's rooms rather than generic product cards.

03

Request layer

The CTA follows the design and measurement logic: choose a direction, leave contacts, describe the room and receive a consultation.

The landing page became a guided route: see the quality, choose a direction, understand the process and request a quote.

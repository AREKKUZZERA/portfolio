01 / Project frame

Development of a corporate identity for a furniture company

The project was completed as a second-year Design degree project at RTU MIREA. The case shows that identity is not limited to a logo and colors: it also defines the website scenario, from first impression to quote request.

02 / Problem

A complex service had to be explained from the first screen

Buying furniture for an interior starts with trust, not with a button. The client does not know the exact price yet, but already needs confidence in the process: measurement, design, materials, production and installation. That is why the page is built around control, quality and calm confidence instead of discounts and loud promises.

01
No ready-made productEvery kitchen, wardrobe or walk-in closet is made for a specific room. The website needed to show individuality without creating uncertainty.
02
Long decision cycleThe user has to understand the stages before submitting a request: measurement, design, materials, production, delivery and installation.
03
Premium without noiseA dark stage, warm typography and large photographs communicate more than overloaded promos and aggressive calls to action.
Photography as evidenceInstead of abstract cards, the website shows texture, scale and a real interior context.
03 / Research

Research findings were translated into interface decisions

The work started with user barriers, not decoration. Each barrier received a concrete answer in the page structure, copy and responsive behavior.

01

Audience

The user chooses furniture for a specific interior and is afraid of mistakes in size, style and budget. The interface therefore shows a managed process, not a product storefront.

02

Barriers

The price becomes clear only after measurement, quality is hard to assess online, and a long form feels like pressure. These points became the basis of the scenario.

03

Solutions

Photography, work stages, a short request form and stable mobile cards help explain the service without overload and preserve the premium feeling.

04 / Strategy

The structure leads from trust to request

First, the visitor reads the brand level, then sees quality proof, chooses the right direction and understands what will happen after the request. The CTA appears only after the user has a reason to trust the company.

01

First screen as a trust showcase

The hero immediately shows the furniture level, the calm brand tone and the main idea: the solution is designed around the client's interior.

02

Proof before choice

Experience, warranty, in-house production and free measurement appear before the catalog to remove key doubts before the request.

03

A form without pressure

The request feels like the start of a consultation: choose a furniture type, leave contact details and receive a clear next step.

05 / Visual system

A dark furniture gallery with a warm accent

The design uses contrast between a dark background and cream typography so the photographs feel more valuable. The gold accent is used with restraint: statuses, lines, CTAs and key numbers.

The typography system separates emotion and interface: the large serif face creates the premium feeling, while the geometric UI type keeps navigation, forms and cards in a clean product structure.

06 / Screens

Key landing page blocks

Each screen answers a separate objection: see the level of work, understand the range, check the process and leave a request without extra steps.

First screen

A large interior image, a short offer and two actions: calculate the price or view the work. No extra promo blocks before trust is established.

Solution catalog

The catalog is not overloaded with SKUs. It shows order types: kitchens, wardrobes, walk-in closets, children's furniture and cabinet solutions.

Process and request

The five-step scheme reduces anxiety: request, measurement, design, production, installation. The final form reinforces the value of the service and collects only the necessary data.

07 / Responsive

The mobile version keeps the showroom feeling

The hero is rebuilt into a vertical scenario: the photo stays central, while copy and CTA do not cover the key texture.
Catalog and process cards keep stable proportions so the screen does not turn into a random feed.
The request form stays short: furniture type, name, phone and comment. This reduces friction on mobile.
